Shanghai Municipal Public Parking Lot (Garage) Parking Rules (Compiled Version)
(Effective until April 14, 2026)

I. General Provisions
These rules are formulated in accordance with the "Shanghai Municipal Parking Lot (Garage) Management Measures" to regulate parking conduct, maintain parking order, and protect legal rights and interests. These rules apply to the parking management activities within the city's public parking lots (garages).

II. Prohibited Entry
Vehicles under any of the following circumstances are not permitted to enter a public parking lot (garage):

The vehicle does not have a license plate or a temporary license plate.
The driver is under the influence of alcohol or refuses to undergo entry registration and safety inspection.
The vehicle is carrying flammable, explosive, toxic, harmful, or other dangerous or prohibited items.
The vehicle has serious safety hazards such as brake failure or oil leakage.
Other situations where specific vehicles are prohibited from parking due to site limitations or temporary control requirements.
III. Obligations of the Parker

Parking Requirements
Park in accordance with signs and markings, and follow the directions of the staff to park within the designated parking space.
When leaving the vehicle, turn off the power, lock the doors and windows, and carry valuable items with you. For cargo vehicles, it is necessary to take reinforcement, anti-theft, and other measures.
Payment of Fees
Pay the parking fee as required. Electronic payment or cash payment is supported. The operator must provide a tax-supervised invoice (electronic/paper) and indicate the download channel.
Use of Facilities
Use the facilities and equipment correctly. Damage caused by personal reasons shall be subject to legal liability.
IV. Prohibited Actions
The following actions are prohibited after entering the parking lot:

Occupying accessible parking spaces, charging facility spaces, or other dedicated parking spaces.
Smoking, using open flames, testing vehicles, littering, unauthorized loading or unloading, or repairing vehicles (in a way that affects the traffic safety of others).
Other acts that violate safety management regulations.
V. Emergency Response and Complaints

In the event of a safety accident or a major hidden danger, the parker must cooperate with the operator's emergency response.
Do not block entrances, exits, or passages.
For objections to charges, services, etc., you may file a complaint with the operator, the citizen hotline (12345), or the traffic management department.
VI. Handling of Violations

Those who cause personal injury or property damage shall bear legal responsibility in accordance with the law.
Those who disrupt public order will be reported to the public security organs for handling; if a crime is constituted, criminal responsibility will be investigated.
